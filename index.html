About me

I am a professional, competent, friendly, and reliable person seeking opportunities to create engaging and impactful experiences to technology users. I am a Ph.D. researcher in the Human-Computer Interaction Group at the University of St Andrews, Scotland, UK. My supervisors are: Trisan Henderson (Primary) and Loraine Clarke (Secondary). My research aims to develop a scalable citizen science framework using a user-centered approach and UX design techniques for researchers who utilise data subject rights as a methodology for data collection.

Research Interest

My research focuses on expanding the scale of research utilising data subject rights as a method for data collection. To achieve that I use a user-centered approach and UX design techniques. As part of my work, I have a solid understanding of Human-Computer Interaction research approaches, Qualitative (thematic analysis) and Quantitative data analysis, Expertise in User experience (UX) research, and User Experience Design. I have developed a framework for researchers that utilises data subject rights as a methodology. My work demonstrates how data subject rights can be employed as a research method from a multi-stakeholder perspective, which includes citizens, researchers, and data protection officers.
